# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Life insurance producers — FFL agents who dial leads, track policies, quote rates, and calculate commissions daily. They work fast, they're competitive, and they evaluate tools by one metric: does it make them more money. They're not tech-averse, but they're not developers either. They need a tool that gets out of the way and helps them close.

## Product Purpose

Producer Stack is the all-in-one CRM built exclusively for life insurance producers. It replaces five disconnected tools (dialer, CRM, rate quoter, tracker, calculator) with one platform. Power Dialer, Lead CRM, live multi-carrier rate quotes, AI Underwriter, KPI dashboard. Success looks like a producer launching it on Monday morning and running their entire book of business from it without touching anything else.

## Brand Personality

Modern. Fast. Empowering. The tool that makes top producers feel like they have an unfair advantage — not through hype, but through execution. Every screen should feel like it was built by someone who has sat on a dialer.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS blue** (Salesforce, HubSpot) — the corporate enterprise CRM look is exactly what Producer Stack replaces; avoid the over-familiar blue-on-white dashboard aesthetic
- **Hustle-bro / MLM energy** — no gold trophies, no "GRIND MODE" copy, no leaderboard-worship; producers are professionals, not hype-train passengers
- **Boring fintech** (gray / navy / cautious) — the flat, compliance-first aesthetic of traditional financial software makes agents feel trapped; Producer Stack is the opposite
- **Overhyped AI startup** — no orbiting particle clouds, no neon-on-black cyberpunk gradients, no "AI-powered" badge on every surface; the intelligence should feel embedded, not performed

## Design Principles

1. **Tool confidence, not hype** — every visual element earns its place by making producers faster or smarter; nothing decorative that doesn't serve the workflow
2. **Speed is felt, not just stated** — interactions should feel instant; layout should reward fast decision-making, not slow page reads
3. **Craft is the proof** — the quality of execution is itself the trust signal; a producer trusts a tool that looks like it was built by someone who gets the job
4. **Industry-specific or it's generic** — the visual language should only be able to come from life insurance context; nothing borrowed from a generic SaaS template
5. **Make the producer feel capable** — every screen should reinforce the sense that the person using it is ahead of the competition

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal compliance target. Best-effort WCAG AA: readable contrast (≥4.5:1 body text), keyboard-navigable core flows, and no motion that can't be reduced. Life insurance producers use Producer Stack on laptops in offices and cars — optimize for bright-light legibility alongside the dark theme.
